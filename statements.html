Logic through Pictures

True statements

Mathematics is especially interested in statements that are true, particularly statements that are true by mathematical reasoning, in other words theorems.

As an example let's consider the statement that every number is either less than 100 or greater than 10. We could state this as x < 100 or x > 10. A picture of the statement might look like this:

In any case the statement x < 100 or x > 10 is true regardless of the value of x. In mathematics a statement like this that is true regardless of the values assigned to its variables is considered a true statement.

Implication -- "implies"

Let's look at another typical kind of statement often seen in mathematics, the sort like "all men are mortal". In our notation we can write (man x) implies (mortal x). We might also express this as "if x is a man then x is mortal".
