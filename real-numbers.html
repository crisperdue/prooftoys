Working with real numbers

At Prooftoys currently has primitive support for working with real numbers using addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. In fact the axioms refer only to addition, multiplication, negation (neg), and reciprocal (recip). These axioms are displayed below in proof form. The predicate R means that its argument is a real number. There are also axioms stating that the result of the basic operations on real numbers give real numbers as a result, except for taking the reciprocal of zero.

In addition to these there is an axiom for actually doing arithmetic on numbers. Prooftoys supports (integer) real number constants in the usual syntax, e.g. (x - 2). Just be sure when subtracting a numeric constant as in the example, leave a space between the - and the number, otherwise the parser will the - as part of the number -2 and x as a function applied to -2.

In the axioms, the turnstile symbol ⊢ means that the items to its left are hypotheses and the expression on the right is proved with the hypotheses assumed. In the prooftoys logic, the turnstile has exactly the same meaning as the implication symbol -->, though some rules of inference distinguish between inputs with hypotheses and those without them.

Finally there is an axiom schema axiomArithmetic for evaluating basic arithmetic expressions. It works with the operations +, -, *, /, neg, =, !=, >, >=, <, <=, and the predicate R when given numeric literals as arguments. (When dividing, it accepts only inputs that give exact integer results.)
