About Functions

Functions express dependencies. A function expresses a dependency of a value on some other values. In the real world we encounter situations in different places at different times and from different points of view. A situation has many observable or measurable values, such as color, sound, direction, distance, quantity, duration, temperature, odor, texture, and so on.

For example, if the angle of the sun above the horizon at some location depends on the time of year, the time of day, that angle is a mathematical function of the time of year and the time of day.

In Q0 relationships are treated as functions with values of true or false. If objects are in a particular relationship, the value of the relationship function is true. If they are not in that relationship, the value is false.
